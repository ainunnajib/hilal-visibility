About These Maps

🔬 Methodology

These maps use the Shaukat Criterion developed by Khalid Shaukat, based on extensive analysis of historical crescent sighting data. The criterion considers moon altitude, crescent width, and observing conditions to predict visibility zones.

📊 Zone Classification

  • Zone A (Red): Crescent easily visible to naked eye under good conditions
  • Zone B (Magenta): Visible to naked eye only under perfect conditions
  • Zone C (Cyan): Optical aid needed to locate, then visible to naked eye
  • Zone D (Blue): Visible only with optical aid (binoculars/telescope)

🔶 Turkey 2016 / KHGT Criterion

The yellow line represents the Turkey 2016 criterion (also known as KHGT), which requires both elongation ≥ 8° AND moon altitude ≥ 5°. This is a simpler, more conservative criterion used by some astronomical organizations.

📅 Observation Dates

Each map shows visibility for the evening before the new Hijri month begins. This is when Muslim communities traditionally look for the new crescent moon to determine the start of lunar months, especially for religious observances like Ramadan and Eid celebrations.

⚠️ Important Notes

  • These are theoretical predictions based on astronomical calculations
  • Actual sighting depends on local weather, atmospheric conditions, and observer experience
  • Always consult local religious authorities for official moon sighting announcements
  • Maps use a 2° grid resolution for computational efficiency
